Multiplication

What is Multiplication? How do you do it?

Multiplication is when you take one amount and you count it a certain number of times. It is represented by both a x sign and a * sign. The symbols are used interchangeably. The numbers being multiplied are called factors. The first factor is the multiplicand, and the second factor is the multiplier. The answer is the product.

Note that anything multiplied by 0 is equal to 0.

If you have the number 4, and you multiply it by 3, you are basically taking 4 and adding it 3 times. 4 * 3 = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12.

Here's another example. Let's say you have a bunch of three bananas. Each bunch is represented by a green box in the picture below. If you had 3 bunches of 3 bananas, then you would have 9 bananas in total. Therefore, 3 * 3 = 9.

Multiplication Table

The grid of numbers you see below is a multiplication table. A multiplication table is a grid of numbers that allows you to see the products of different factors. You can do this by starting from the top-left of the table, where there is an empty space. Move to the right until you have a desired multiplicand. Then, go back to the top-left. Now, move down until you have a desired multiplier. Now, from the multiplicand on top, move your finger down. From the multiplier to the left, move your other finger to the right. Do this until your fingers touch. The point where they meet is the product.
